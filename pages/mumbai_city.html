Gateway of India

            

About:

The Gateway of India is one of Mumbai’s most famous landmarks. It was built in 1924 to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to India in 1911. Located at Apollo Bunder, near the Taj Mahal Palace Hotel, it overlooks the Arabian Sea.
The structure is designed in Indo-Saracenic architecture, made of yellow basalt and reinforced concrete. It is about 26 meters (85 feet) high. After India gained independence in 1947, the last British troops left India through this gateway, making it a symbol of both colonial legacy and freedom. Today, it is a popular tourist attraction and starting point for ferry rides to Elephanta Caves and other nearby islands. The area around the Gateway is always lively with street vendors, photographers, and sea views.

Siddhivinayak Temple

            

About:

The Siddhivinayak Temple is one of Mumbai’s most famous and wealthy temples dedicated to Lord Ganesha. Located in Prabhadevi, Mumbai, it was originally built in 1801 by Laxman Vithu and Deubai Patil. The main idol is carved from a single black stone, about 2.5 feet high, with the trunk turned to the right (considered very auspicious). Thousands of devotees visit daily; Tuesdays are especially crowded. Many celebrities and politicians also visit for blessings.
The temple’s idol of Lord Ganesha is unique — it is two and a half feet high, carved out of a single black stone, with the trunk turned to the right, which is considered very sacred and powerful. Every day, thousands of devotees visit this temple, and on Tuesdays, there are especially long queues of devotees.

Juhu Beach

            

About:

Juhu Beach is one of the most famous and populated beaches in Mumbai, located in the suburb of Juhu on the western coast. It’s a popular spot for locals and tourists alike, especially in the evenings.
The beach is known for its sunsets, street food stalls, and vibrant local atmosphere. Famous foods to try: bhelpuri, sev puri, pani puri, vada pav, among others. There are many Bollywood celebrities living nearby, which adds to its glamour. Juhu Beach is also used for film shoots and events occasionally. Best time to visit is early morning or after sunset when it’s cooler and less crowded.
